## 1. Information the App uses and stores

### 1.1 Information stored on your device

The App uses a **local database** on your device. Depending on how you use ClearVision, this may include:

| Type | Examples | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Block list** | App identifiers (e.g. Android package names), display names, optional icon references, and on iOS **opaque tokens** required by Apple’s APIs to apply shields | To remember which apps you asked to block |
| **Settings** | Key–value preferences, including an **optional paired NFC tag identifier** if you use a physical key | To operate blocking, focus mode, and NFC features |
| **Focus sessions** | Start/end times and duration summaries | To show focus statistics and history in the App |

This data **stays on your device** unless you back up your phone (e.g. iCloud or Google backup), in which case it may be included in your **device backup** under your platform’s rules—we do not control that.

### 1.2 Permissions and system access

To provide blocking and related features, the App may ask for permissions such as:

- **Android:** Accessibility-related access, foreground service, system alert window (overlay), and **NFC** if you use a tag.
- **iOS:** **Family Controls / Screen Time**–style authorization and related entitlements so the system can shield selected apps.

These permissions are used **to run the features you enable**. The App is designed **not** to read the **content** of other apps (for example, message text or keystrokes) for analytics; see in-app disclosures for how each permission is described on your platform.

### 1.3 Information you send to us

If you **email us** or otherwise contact us, we receive whatever you choose to share (for example, your email address and the content of your message). We use that only to **respond** and improve support, unless a separate legal basis applies.

### 1.4 Information we do not collect through the App

We do **not** require you to create an account in the App. We do **not** intentionally collect special categories of data (such as racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, or sexual orientation) through the App.
